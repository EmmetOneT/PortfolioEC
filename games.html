Drone Annihilation: Blacksite

My first-person shooter was made in Unreal Engine entirely using Blueprints. The game can be played with a mouse and keyboard but also has full gamepad support. There is a graphics settings menu with all the options expected from a modern game, from texture quality to anti-aliasing. An optimal settings button detects the user's hardware and adjusts the graphics accordingly. The player can save these settings. The game includes a timer in which the player must destroy all drones on the map to win. It is designed as an old-school shooter, featuring ammunition, health, and time pickups. If you get too close to a drone, it will navigate towards you before exploding. If a drone is shot but not destroyed, it will aggro and navigate towards the player.

An Uafáis - The Horror

"An Uafáis - The Horror" was my final project for Game Development at ATU Sligo. Made in Unity using Playmaker and C#, it is a PlayStation 1-style horror game reminiscent of Resident Evil. The player navigates through a mansion infested with zombie-like enemies. The player will find a flashlight that requires batteries. The battery meter on the HUD slowly depletes, changing from green to orange to red, and the light progressively dims. They will also find a pistol that needs ammunition. The regular enemies have damage-sensitive hitboxes: one shot to the head, 3-5 to the body, and more to the limbs to stop them. There are riddles and puzzles throughout the game that the player must solve to progress. The final boss teleports and despawns randomly around the player in a final battle in the maze of furniture in the mansion's attic.

Unreal Obstacle

"Unreal Obstacle" is a third-person platformer written entirely in C++ in Unreal Engine. Climb to the top of the level, navigating moving obstacles such as spinning platforms, rising and falling platforms, and pushing blocks. There is a checkpoint system in place. When the player gets past an obstacle section but falls off the world, they will be respawned at a point before the next obstacle.

Music Room Wreckage

"Music Room Wreckage" is a physics-based destruction game made in Unreal Engine using C++. The player starts in a music room where every object has physics and gravity attached. The player can navigate the room and shoot balls. The player can shoot 30 balls to destroy the room. When all the balls are used, the room will reset.
